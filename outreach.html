And Now For Some Animal Facts!

Assortment of species I've had the pleasure of meeting along the way.

Loggerhead turtle, Belize

Loggerheads can take up to 39 years to reach sexual maturity.

Spotted ratfish, Bamfield

Like sharks and rays, chimaeras like the spotted ratfish have skeletons made of cartilage rather than bone.

Sea otter, Salish Sea

Sea otters are the largest member of the weasel family .

California sealion, Race Rocks

California sea lions are the fastest of all seals and sea lions, reaching speeds of 40 kmph.

Male Orca, Salish Sea

Male orca dorsal fins can reach up to six feet.

Manta, Labuan Bajo

These gentle giants can weigh as much as 3,000 lb and have wingspans of up to 7 m!

Alabaster nudibranch, Victoria

While these nudibranchs might appear harmless, they are a predator that feeds on snails by cracking the shells with their jaws.

Caterpillar, Manu Jungle, Peru

Peru has the most species of butterflies in the world (around 3,700)!.

Humpback whales, Barkley Sound

Despite their massive size, humpbacks can only swallow food roughly smaller than the size of a human fist.

Chestnut-fronted Macaw, Peru

Other than humans, the main predator of these birds are boa constrictors.

Brown fur seal, Port Phillip Bay

Brown fur seals in Australia will gather into colonies ranging from 500-1500 individuals.
